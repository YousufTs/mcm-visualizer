📘 Educational Content & CLO Breakdown

CLO-3: Design Paradigm (Dynamic Programming)

This algorithm uses Dynamic Programming. Instead of calculating the same matrix multiplications repeatedly (like Brute Force), we solve smaller subproblems (chains of length 2, then 3...) and store their optimal answers in the table above. Calculating m[1,4] reuses the already computed values of m[1,2], m[3,4], demonstrating overlapping subproblems.

CLO-2: Role of Searching

Searching is used to find the optimal split point k. For every chain from Ai to Aj, the algorithm systematically searches through all possible positions of k (where i ≤ k < j) to find the split that yields the minimum number of scalar multiplications.

CLO-1: Asymptotic Analysis & Complexity

Time Complexity: O(n³) due to 3 nested loops (l, i, k).
Space Complexity: O(n²) to store the m-table and s-table.
